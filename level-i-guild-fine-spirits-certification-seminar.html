Level I: Guild Fine Spirits Seminar

View Dates, Locations and Register for the Fine Spirits Seminar

Better understand and appreciate fine spirits with this introductory, professional caliber Fine Spirits Seminar. 

You will develop a basic knowledge of fine spirits terms and concepts, production techniques, technical and description evaluation, and spirits and food pairing concepts.

Seminar Overview:

  • Taste 23+ fine spirits, including Scotch, Bourbon, Vodka and Brandies
  • Evaluate a 4-course spirits and food dinner to learn about spirits and food pairing
  • Receive 100 pages of educational material designed specifically for this seminar 
  • Earn a diploma, IWSG membership card and IWSG membership pin
  • Meet other fine spirits enthusiasts 

This is not a mixology seminar - it is a seminar to learn about fine spirits (consumed neat or straight). 

Certification 

Depending upon your background and professional needs, we offer the option of earning a Fine Spirits Sommelier certification (for the professional) or a Fine Spirits Connoisseur certification (for the enthusiast). 

Course Approvals

Professional programs of the International Wine Guild are approved and regulated by the Colorado Department of Higher Education, Private Occupational School Board.

What type of people have completed the fine spirits seminar?

This Fine Spirits Seminar can be taken for personal enrichment or professional certification, and has been taken by Executive Chefs, Sous Chefs, Banquet Chefs, Chefs, Cooks, Restaurant Owners, Importers, Distributors, Wine Buyers, Appraisers, Retail Liquor Store Staff and Owners, Sommeliers, Waitstaff, Meeting and Banquet Planners, Convention Managers, Catering Directors, Hospitality Managers, Doctors, Lawyers, Real Estate Brokers, Bankers, Culinary Students, and anyone interested in fine spirits!

If you are interested in joining the Guild and becoming certified as a professional in the industry or a serious enthusiast then you should check out the Professional Program requirements.
