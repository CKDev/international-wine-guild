Fermentation

Wine School & Wine Classes—Common Wine Terminology for the Beginner

Blind tasting
Rating a wine based on its elements without knowing its origin.

New World Wines
North and South America, New Zealand and Australia are presented as New World winemakers. Winemaking traditions traveled from Old World grape-growing regions to the New World.

Old World Wines
Old World wines can be traced back to monasteries and family vineyards in European nations of France, Germany, Italy, and Spain.

Old World wines are known to be more delicate to taste than New World wines. Reds have a propensity for being softer than what are coined, “Big Reds” or gallant red wines typically originating from the New World.

Viniculture
Viniculture is the authority study of winemaking. Experts of winemaking are called viniculturists.

Viniculturists studying Organic Viticulture (the study of grape growing) stress natural grape growing methods. This type of harvesting relies on fewer chemicals and fertilizers for pest control.

Vintage
Vintage denotes the grape growing year of the wine.

Vintner
A vintner is a maker or seller of wine; a wine vendor.

Yield
The yield is the amount of wine or grapes generated in a season.
